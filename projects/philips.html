Machine Learning & Sleep

Philips | May 2018 - May 2018

After my second year in the University of Pittsburgh's Biomedical Informatics PhD program, I interned at Philips in Pittsburgh, PA. I worked on 2 projects in a machine learning scientist capactiy and spearheaded a third project. After working as a full time intern for over 3 months, I continued interning part-time while continuing the third year of my PhD program.


SmartSleep (Demo'ed at CES 2019)

  • Analyzed data from over 1000 users to identify shared complaints associated with common sleep problems
  • Visualized data patterns using unsupervised ML (agglomerative clustering/heatmaps)
  • Built deep learning frameworks to predict a user's sleep problems based on their responses to a clinically-validated survey
  • Developed and presented a plan to incorporate and expand the ML abilities of the project
  • Co-authored 7 invention disclosures


Smart Home Sleep Assistant

  • Explored the smart home/virtual assistant domain and evaluated existing technologies
  • Identified software and human-computer interface requirements
  • Utilized Rasa and Actions on Google to build a sleep-related conversational user interface (CUI)
  • Presented a live demo of the CUI of to department leaders as well as an international team from a different department
  • Worked with department members to identify other applications of a CUI in the company


Natural Language Sleep Terminology

  • Lead this project on a team of 3 interns
  • Organized free-text responses from a survey of over 1000 users into general categories
  • Developed a set of contextual standards for specifying concepts associated with different phrases
  • Collaborated with 2 team members to manually annotate over 900 instances of free-text sleep complaints
  • Investigated natural language processing techniques to better differentiate between similar phrases
  • Built an ML model to classify the concepts present in a sample of free text
  • Presented the model to the department and modified the concept classification based on expert feedback
  • Wrote 1 invention disclosure around this project
  • Incorporated the model into the Smart Home Sleep Assistant
