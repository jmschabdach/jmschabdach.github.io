Identifying Similar COPD Patients: An Imaging Approach

Batman Lab, University of Pittsburgh | September 2016 - May 2017

During my first two semesters as a PhD student in the University of Pittsburgh's Biomedical Informatics program, I worked with Dr. Kayhan Batmanghelich on a project to identify similar COPD (chronic obstructive pulmonary disease) patients.

COPD is an umbrella term for people who have progressive lung diseases including emphysema, chronic bronchitis, and non-reversible asthma. Patients who suffer from COPD have increasing difficulty breathing, though the treatments to prevent disease progression depend on the underlying causes. Computed Tomography (CT) imaging is often used to take detailed pictures of the patient's lungs to identify the different subtypes of the disease present. We built a pipeline to compare CT scans from a large population (N=6253?) of COPD patients. The pipeline not only identifies patients with similar COPD presentations, but also highlights areas of the lungs which contribute to their classification.
