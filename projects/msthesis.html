Image Registration for Breast Cancer

Applied Informatics Group/ECE Department, Drexel University | Jan 2015 - June 2016

Approximately 250,000 people are diagnosed with breast cancer each year. It is the second leading cause of death in the United States. The standard of care for breast cancer screening is bilateral mammograms taken every two years. If a mammogram is inconclusive, the patient undergoes a magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scan. MRI scans are expensive, though, and may not be financially accessible for all patients. Other imaging modalities that use radiation are not advisable for potential cancer patients. Volumetric ultrasounds, however, may be a reasonable supplementary screening method for complex breast cancer cases.

The goal of this project was to describe a pipeline for combining mammogram and volumetric ulrasound images in the same image space. My proof of concept pipeline showed that mammograms and volumetric ultraounds could be registered to potentially increase a doctor's confidence in a breast cancer diagnosis prior to a biopsy. As a follow up, I identified weak points in the registration pipeline and developed a plan to address them.
